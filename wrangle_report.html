A Wrangle Report on WeRateDogs¶

WeRateDogs is a Twitter account that rates people's dogs with a humorous comment about the dog. The account was started in 2015 by college student Matt Nelson, and has received international media attention for its popularity.¶

Wrangling Report¶

The wrangling report consists of the quality and tidiness issues that were found in the data and how they were corrected. Two datasets were provided for the analysis, that is Twitter Archive and Dogs Prediction but one was scrapped for WeRateDogs website, which was named Tweet Status. The following are the datasets, the issues found in the dataset, and how they were corrected:¶

Twitter Archive¶

This was a dataset with 2356 rows and 17 columns with the following quality issues:¶

• reply_to_status_id, in_reply_to_user_id, retweeted_status_id, retweeted_status_user_id, retweeted_status_timestamp had huge amounts of nulls and they were deleted¶

• name, doggo, floofer, pupper, and puppo columns had None’s which were replaced with NaN¶

• doggo, floofer, pupper, and puppo columns were concatenated dogs_name for easier analysis.¶

• New day, hour, hour, year were created from the timestamp column.¶

• rating_denominator had values above 10. They were corrected to 10.¶

• rating_denominator was combined with rating_numerator to create one column of ratings by dividing both columns.¶

• Values on dogs_ratings which more than 20 were substituted with NaNs.¶

• There were some rows in the name column which had only a’s and were deleted.¶

Dogs Prediction¶

This was a dataset with 12 columns and 2075 rows with the following quality issues:¶

• p1, p2, and p3 columns values had underscores. The underscores were replaced with spaces.¶

• Some words in the p1, p2, and p3 columns were inconsistently capitalized, and they were corrected by capitalizing both words.¶

• The url.jpg had duplicates which were deleted.¶

Tweet Status¶

This was scrapped from Twitter and had 3 columns and 2326 rows with only one identified issue.¶

• The datatypes of the columns in the Tweet Status dataframe were objects which were converted to int64.¶

Tidiness Issues¶

Twitter Archive, Dogs Prediction, and Tweet Status were combined into one master dataframe. The Dogs Prediction column names were not descriptive and they new column names were created. That is, p1, p1_conf, p1_dog to prediction1, prediction1_confidence, prediction1_dog, etc.¶
